Problem

A client needed a local business dataset for outreach in the Monroe, LA area — names, categories, phone numbers, addresses — across hundreds of businesses. Manual research in directories and maps was slow, inconsistent between researchers, and painful to repeat when the list needed refreshing a month later.

Approach

I built a Python pipeline that pulled from public web directories (Yellow Pages, Google Maps listings, chamber directories), normalized each source into a common schema, and exported a clean CSV plus SQL-ready table.

01

Scrape

Python crawlers extract names, categories, contact info, and locations per source.

02

Clean

Normalize phone formats, standardize addresses, map categories to one taxonomy.

03

Dedupe

Match on business name + phone; drop overlapping entries across sources.

04

Export

Deliver leads_clean.csv and SQL table for segmentation and outreach.

Decision

Multi-source scrape with aggressive deduplication. No single directory had complete coverage. Pulling from three sources and deduplicating on name + phone got better recall than betting on one site — at the cost of 26 duplicate rows removed from the initial 524-record pull.

Deliver a flat file, not a live app. The client needed a list to work from in Excel and a CRM import — not a dashboard. Shipping CSV + SQL kept scope tight and let them start outreach the same week.

Result

Delivered 498 deduplicated business records across 38 categories, ready for client outreach and market segmentation. The pipeline ran end-to-end without manual steps between extraction and export.

Client received a segmented outreach dataset — 498 businesses across 38 categories, formatted for CRM import and local market outreach.
